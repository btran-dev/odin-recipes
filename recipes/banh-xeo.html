Recipe for Banh Xeo

Description

Banh xeo is a Vietnamese dish similar to a savory crepe. It has a crispy, golden/yellow crust and is filled with pork, shrimp, mung beans, onions, and bean sprouts. Banh xeo is often served with fresh herbs and with a side of fish sauce to dip in.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1/2 tsp. white sugar
  • 1/2 tsp. ground tumeric
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tbsp. vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp. micned shallot
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3/4 lb. shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tbsp. fish sauce
  • 1 lb. mung bean sprouts
  • 4 lettuce leaves (use more as needed)
  • salt to taste

Steps

  1. Make the crepe batter: mix rice flour, sugar, salt, tumeric together in a large bowl. Beat in coconut milk until thick. Slowly beat in water until it reaches the consistency of a thin crepe batter.
  2. Make the filling: heat 1 1/2 tbsp. oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add shallot and garlic, then cook until fragrant but not browned (1-2 mins). Add shrimp, then saute until cooked through and opaque (3-4 mins). Season with fish sauce and salt. Transfer filling to a bowl.
  3. Preheat overn to 200F (95C)
  4. Wipe out the skillet and reheat over medium heat. Add remaining 1/2 tbsp. of oil. Stir crepe batter and pour 1/2 cup into the hot skillet, swiling to coat the bottom. Lay 3-4 cooked shrimp on the bottom half of the crepe. Top with a small handful of bean sprouts. Cook until batter looks set and edges start to brown (~1 min). Fold crepe over and slide onto an oven-safe plate.
  5. Place crepe in preheated oven to keep warm. Repeat with remaining batter, shrimp, and bean sprouts.
  6. Serve crepes with lettuce leaves. Break off pieces of crepe and roll them up in lettuce to eat.
